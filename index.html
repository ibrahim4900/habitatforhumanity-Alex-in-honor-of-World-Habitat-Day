Alex,

If you follow the news, you probably know that the cost of housing is rising much faster than the incomes of many families and individuals, adding to an already critical global housing crisis.

According to a recent UN-Habitat report, an estimated 2.8 billion people globally are affected by housing inadequacy. Close to 1.1 billion of those people have been forced to live in slums and informal settlements. Simply put, it isn't fair, it isn't right.

At Habitat for Humanity, we know that it is possible to create a world where everyone has decent, affordable housing, but we have to work together!

For World Habitat Day, we are asking our community to affirm that adequate housing is a basic human right that needs to be celebrated and protected. Do you agree? Click to automatically add your name!

Construction of entry-level homes has steadily declined since the 1970s, putting the dream of homeownership out of reach for many Americans. The rise in construction costs, labor shortages and supply chain disruptions exacerbate the housing shortage.

For households with low incomes, skyrocketing inflation has made the situation even worse. Many find themselves foregoing basic needs — like nutritious food, utility bills and healthcare — to cover the cost of housing.

Every year, World Habitat Day serves as a reminder that everyone deserves to live in a decent, affordable home where they can build a stable, healthy future for themselves and their families.

People like you make such a critical impact on the work we do, Alex. This World Habitat Day, will you join us in recommitting yourself to support the right of all people to have access to affordable housing?

Thank you for your support, and happy World Habitat Day,

Habitat for Humanity

Source: 2023 State of the Nation’s Housing Report, Harvard University’s Joint Center for Housing Studies
